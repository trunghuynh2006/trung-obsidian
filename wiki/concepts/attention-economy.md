# Attention Economy

## Definition

An economic system in which human attention is the scarce resource being bought, sold, and competed for. Digital platforms — social media, news feeds, video — generate revenue by maximizing time-on-platform, incentivizing them to engineer interruption, novelty, and compulsion at the expense of users' focus.

## How It Works Against Focus

Every notification, infinite scroll, and algorithmic recommendation is engineered by behavioral scientists to capture and hold attention. The platforms aren't broken — they're working exactly as designed. That design is fundamentally misaligned with sustained focus.

Key data points [[wiki/sources/deep-work-flow-state]]:
- Average person spends ~7 hours/day on digital devices.
- Average screen attention span: **47 seconds** (down from 2.5 minutes in 2004).
- 79% of workers can't go 1 hour without distraction; 60% can't manage 30 minutes.

Modern work culture compounds this: responsiveness (fast replies, always-on) is often rewarded more visibly than deep output. Fragmented attention becomes the default mode.

## The Opportunity

Newport's argument: because the attention economy has made deep focus so rare, those who can cultivate it gain a genuine competitive advantage. The rarity of the skill inflates its value.
