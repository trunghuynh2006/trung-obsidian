# Robotics Career Paths

## Definition

The range of professional roles within the robotics industry, spanning hardware, software, research, field work, and commercial functions. Robotics careers are notably diverse because the field is multidisciplinary — there is no single "robotics job."

## Industry Overview (as of 2026)

- **Market size**: $43.32 billion projected by 2027 [[wiki/sources/robotics-for-beginners-playtolabs]]
- **Average salary** (US, robotics engineer): $117,240/year [[wiki/sources/robotics-for-beginners-playtolabs]]
- **Unemployment rate**: 2.3% vs. 4.83% national average [[wiki/sources/robotics-for-beginners-playtolabs]]
- **Job growth**: 6.4% expansion projected by 2026; 7% demand growth through 2030 [[wiki/sources/robotics-for-beginners-playtolabs]]

## Career Paths

| Role | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Robotics Engineer** | Design, build, test robots — manufacturing, healthcare, defense |
| **AI/ML Specialist** | Algorithms for autonomous decision-making and learning |
| **Robotics Technician** | Maintenance and repair — electronics, mechanics, software |
| **Research Scientist** | Novel robotic technologies and capabilities |
| **Robotics System Designer** | Overall architecture of integrated robotic solutions |
| **Robotics Software Engineer** | Software controlling robot operations |
| **Field Service Technician** | Install, maintain, repair robots at customer sites |
| **Sales Engineer** | Sell robot systems; identify and match customer needs |

## Entry Points by Background

From [[wiki/sources/robotics-getting-started]]:
- ME → strong in mechanical design and dynamics
- EE → strong in electronics, sensing, power
- CS → strong in software, perception, AI

No single entry point is correct. Career path often depends on which discipline you enter through.
