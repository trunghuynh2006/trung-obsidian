# Progressive Summarization

## Definition

A resource preparation technique from Tiago Forte's BASB. A multi-pass highlighting method that makes source material easier to skim and retrieve later — without moving beyond the resource into actual idea extraction.

## The Four Passes

1. **File** — capture and organize the resource (counts as first processing).
2. **Bold** — read and bold passages that catch your eye.
3. **Highlight** — from what's bolded, highlight what seems most important.
4. **Executive summary** — list key points at the top of the document.

## What It Is and Isn't

Progressive summarization is a method of **preparing resources**, not processing knowledge. At every pass, you remain within the source. You are making it easier to skim later — not extracting ideas, forming connections, or doing analysis.

Forte explicitly discourages deep analysis during this step to protect project-oriented flow:

> "Don't worry about analyzing, interpreting, or categorizing each point to decide whether to highlight it. That is way too taxing and will break the flow of your concentration. Instead, rely on your intuition." — Forte

This is pragmatic for project workers who need to move resources quickly. But it means **the actual knowledge work must happen elsewhere** — in the manuscript, in conversation, in a Zettelkasten.

## Contrast with Zettelkasten Processing

| Progressive Summarization | Zettelkasten Processing |
|---|---|
| Stays within the resource | Leaves the resource; extracts atomic ideas |
| Highlights important passages | Reformulates ideas in your own words |
| Prepares for later retrieval | Integrates ideas into the existing network |
| Fast, low friction | Slow, forces understanding |
| Resource management | Knowledge work |
