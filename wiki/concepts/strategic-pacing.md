# Strategic Pacing

## Definition

Moving with disciplined speed under uncertainty rather than waiting for perfect clarity. Strategic pacing rejects both paralysis and reckless acceleration: the goal is informed direction, clear ownership, and just-in-time momentum in conditions where volatility is the norm.

## Detail

In the current source set, strategic pacing is the organizational counterpart to AI-era volatility. The KPMG-backed article argues that business environments now change too quickly for traditional certainty-seeking governance to work well. Planning cycles compress, AI opens and closes opportunities faster, and the old logic of waiting for "normal" conditions becomes a hidden form of strategic inaction.

The practical implication is that organizations should optimize for maneuverability rather than forecast precision. The source offers four operating principles:
- fund measurable outcomes rather than precommitted schedules
- replace consensus-seeking with trusted adaptability and clear decision owners
- redesign workflows with an AI-native mindset instead of automating existing complexity
- cultivate a bias for action based on good-but-not-perfect information

This concept belongs near the wiki's AI + labor and organizational-design branch. It does not answer the [[wiki/concepts/expertise-paradox]] question of what AI does to particular professions. Instead, it asks how firms should govern themselves while those profession-level shifts are unfolding.

## Evidence & Examples

- KPMG's analysis of 1,800-plus U.S. public companies is cited as finding 4.4x higher total shareholder returns and nearly 3x revenue growth for firms that accelerated transformation during uncertainty [[wiki/sources/this-is-the-biggest-risk-a-company-can-take-during-the-age-of-ai]]
- The article's Formula One metaphor captures the logic: leaders tap the brakes, choose a line, and accelerate without waiting for full visibility [[wiki/sources/this-is-the-biggest-risk-a-company-can-take-during-the-age-of-ai]]
- "You can't automate a mess" is the source's concise expression of why workflow redesign must precede AI layering [[wiki/sources/this-is-the-biggest-risk-a-company-can-take-during-the-age-of-ai]]
