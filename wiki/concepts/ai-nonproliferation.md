# AI Nonproliferation

## Definition

The attempt to limit the spread of frontier AI systems whose capabilities are judged dangerous enough that broad access would sharply increase large-scale security risk, especially in cybersecurity and critical infrastructure.

## Detail

In the current source set, AI nonproliferation appears as an analogy to nuclear nonproliferation, but applied to highly cyber-capable models rather than weapons material. The core concern is that some frontier systems may not merely improve ordinary productivity. They may radically lower the cost of sophisticated cyberattack capability by making vulnerability discovery and exploitation accessible to much smaller actors.

The proposed response in the Mythos/Glasswing source has three layers:
- **controlled release** — restrict access to a limited set of trusted governments and companies
- **defensive acceleration** — use the temporary access asymmetry to find and patch vulnerabilities before broader diffusion
- **international coordination** — treat model containment as a geopolitical problem, not only a company policy problem

This concept sits at the boundary of [[wiki/concepts/ai-cybersecurity]] and [[wiki/concepts/ai-governance]]. AI cybersecurity explains the dual-use capability itself. AI governance explains organizational responsibility. AI nonproliferation asks a further question: when a model appears to democratize offensive cyber capability at scale, should access to it be managed like a strategic technology rather than an ordinary software product?

## Evidence & Examples

- Anthropic reportedly restricted Claude Mythos Preview to a consortium of roughly 40 technology companies and critical-infrastructure operators rather than releasing it generally [[wiki/sources/anthropic-ai-claude-mythos]]
- The article says Mythos found thousands of high-severity vulnerabilities, including some in every major operating system and web browser [[wiki/sources/anthropic-ai-claude-mythos]]
- Craig Mundie is quoted arguing that cyberattack capability may be democratized from large states and organizations down to small actors [[wiki/sources/anthropic-ai-claude-mythos]]
- The source explicitly invokes nuclear nonproliferation as the closest geopolitical analogy for the kind of coordination now needed [[wiki/sources/anthropic-ai-claude-mythos]]

## Contradictions

No direct counter-source yet, but the current argument assumes that access controls can meaningfully delay proliferation and that "trusted" actors can be clearly identified. Neither claim is demonstrated in the source set so far.
