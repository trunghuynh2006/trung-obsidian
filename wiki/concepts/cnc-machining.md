# CNC Machining

## Definition

CNC machining is a subtractive manufacturing process in which computer-controlled machine tools remove material from a workpiece to produce precise, repeatable parts with tight tolerances.

## Detail

In the current source set, CNC machining sits at the intersection of classic precision manufacturing and newer connected-factory ideas. Its traditional value is accuracy, repeatability, and the ability to move from prototype to production without changing the basic process logic. Its newer value comes from integration with sensors, software, and adaptive control systems that make machining cells more observable and, in some cases, more responsive during the run itself.

The aluminum-machining source adds an important material-specific lens: CNC is not just about the machine, but about the pairing between machine and material. Aluminum is framed as especially attractive because it cuts quickly, wears tooling less aggressively than harder metals, supports tight tolerances, and offers a strong strength-to-weight tradeoff. The robotics-trends source adds a complementary boundary point: high-precision machining is no longer only a standalone CNC story, but is increasingly adjacent to robotics and connected automation ecosystems.

## Evidence & Examples

- Aluminum is presented as a preferred CNC material because it supports fast cutting, complex geometries, corrosion resistance, recyclability, and tolerances down to roughly 0.025 mm [[wiki/sources/the-growing-demand-for-aluminum-cnc-machining-in-modern-industries]]
- Aerospace, automotive, construction, consumer electronics, energy, and medical manufacturing are all named as domains relying on CNC-machined aluminum parts [[wiki/sources/the-growing-demand-for-aluminum-cnc-machining-in-modern-industries]]
- The 2026 robotics trends source identifies high-precision machining as an emerging connected-automation area, noting that new robotic systems are beginning to encroach on work historically associated with CNC machines [[wiki/sources/robotics-trends-2026]]
