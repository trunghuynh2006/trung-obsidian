## Key Points

- Deep work = focus without distraction on a difficult task; can make you up to **500% more productive** (HBR, 2014).
- **15–20 minutes** to reach a productive flow state; every interruption resets the clock.
- Average employee interrupted **31.6 times/day** — every ~15 minutes.
- Multitasking reduces productivity by **40%**.
- Only **11.2 hours/week** of productive work on average; workers waste 2 hours/day on unproductive tasks.
- Only **53.5%** of planned tasks completed per week.
- **25.6 meetings/week** average; **71%** of meetings unproductive.
- **60.2%** burnout rate among full-time employees.
- Average workweek: 47.6 hours — yet most of it is shallow.
- Deep work limit: **4–5 hours/day** (consistent with prior source).
- Newport's 4 rules: (1) Work deeply, (2) Embrace boredom, (3) Quit social media, (4) Drain the shallows.
- 8-step practice: organize tasks by depth, set deep work hours, time block, block distractions, optimize meetings, integrate PM tools, take breaks, track metrics.
- Time blocking can improve productivity by up to **80%**.
- 1–2 deep work tasks per day in 1–4 hour sessions is a practical target.
