# Norma McCorvey

**Type:** person
**Also known as:** Jane Roe

## Overview

Norma McCorvey was the plaintiff behind *Roe v. Wade* (1973), the US Supreme Court ruling that legalized abortion nationwide. She was a 21-year-old poor, uneducated, unmarried woman in Texas who sought an abortion in 1970; her case was adopted by lawyers seeking a vehicle to challenge Texas's abortion ban. Her name was anonymized as "Jane Roe." By the time the Supreme Court ruled in her favor on January 22, 1973, it was too late for her own abortion; she gave birth and placed the child for adoption. (Years later she became a pro-life activist.)

Freakonomics uses her case as the central causal link in the 1990s crime drop: the ruling she was the face of eliminated a significant cohort of children who would statistically have been at highest risk of criminal activity. The effect was demographic, delayed 18–20 years, and invisible in real time — the paradigm example of a distant, subtle cause with a dramatic delayed effect.
