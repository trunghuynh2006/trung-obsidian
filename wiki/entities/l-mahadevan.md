# L. Mahadevan

**Type:** person
**Also known as:** Lakshminarayanan Mahadevan

## Overview

L. Mahadevan is the Lola England de Valpine Professor of Applied Mathematics, Organismic and Evolutionary Biology, and Physics at [[wiki/entities/harvard-seas]] and the Harvard Faculty of Arts and Sciences. His lab works at the intersection of physics, biology, and mathematics, studying physical phenomena that orchestrate complex natural processes — from ant colonies to brain folds to gut morphology.

Mahadevan has studied social insect communities for many years. Earlier work (2022) showed that the excavation and escape behaviors of ant colonies could be mimicked using simple robots. His 2026 *PRX Life* paper extends this to a full build-and-dismantle swarm with an identified parameter space for emergent behaviors. He coined the term [[wiki/concepts/exbodied-intelligence]] to describe collective cognition distributed into an evolving environment rather than located in individual agents.

## Key Facts

- Coined "exbodied intelligence" — collective cognition arising from agent-environment interaction, not individual capability [[wiki/sources/simple-robots-ants-excavate]]
- Led development of the RAnts (robotic ants) platform that uses photormones (light fields) instead of chemical pheromones [[wiki/sources/simple-robots-ants-excavate]]
- Published swarm robotics excavation/escape work in 2022; extended to construction + dismantling in 2026 [[wiki/sources/simple-robots-ants-excavate]]
- Holds a joint appointment across Applied Mathematics, Organismic and Evolutionary Biology, and Physics [[wiki/sources/simple-robots-ants-excavate]]
