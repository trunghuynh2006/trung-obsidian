# Microsoft PL-900

**Type:** product/certification
**Also known as:** PL-900, Power Platform Fundamentals

## Overview

Microsoft PL-900 is the **Power Platform Fundamentals** exam — the entry-level certification for the entire Power Platform suite. It is a breadth exam, not a depth exam: it tests conceptual understanding of what each product does, when to use it, and how the components connect, rather than requiring hands-on developer competence.

The target audience is business analysts, project managers, IT decision-makers, and anyone building a case for or getting started with Power Platform. No coding is required. The exam rewards people who can match a business scenario to the correct Power Platform tool and describe its capabilities accurately.

PL-900 is also the gateway credential before higher-level certifications such as PL-200 (Functional Consultant), PL-400 (Developer), or PL-600 (Solution Architect). It establishes the shared vocabulary and conceptual foundation the other exams build on.

## Skill Domains

The exam is organized into six domains (approximate weights):

| Domain | Weight |
|---|---|
| Describe the business value of Microsoft Power Platform | 15–20% |
| Identify the core components of Microsoft Power Platform | 10–15% |
| Demonstrate the capabilities of Power BI | 15–20% |
| Demonstrate the capabilities of Power Apps | 15–20% |
| Demonstrate the capabilities of Power Automate | 15–20% |
| Demonstrate the capabilities of Microsoft Copilot Studio | 10–15% |

## Key Conceptual Areas

- **Power Platform value proposition**: productivity, low-code, integration with Microsoft 365 and Azure
- **Dataverse**: what it is, how it relates to tables/columns/rows, security model at a conceptual level, business rules
- **Power BI**: reports vs. dashboards, datasets, workspaces, sharing, premium vs. free licensing
- **Power Apps**: canvas apps vs. model-driven apps vs. Power Pages — when to use each
- **Power Automate**: cloud flows, desktop flows (RPA), triggers, approvals, connectors
- **Microsoft Copilot Studio**: chatbot creation, topics, entities, actions, channel publishing
- **Connectors**: standard vs. premium connectors, licensing implications
- **Microsoft 365 integration**: Teams, SharePoint, Outlook as Power Platform entry points
- **AI Builder**: prebuilt models, custom models, use cases within Power Apps and Power Automate
