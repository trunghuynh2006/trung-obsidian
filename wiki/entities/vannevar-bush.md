# Vannevar Bush

**Type:** person
**Era:** 1890–1974

## Overview

American engineer, inventor, and science administrator. Director of the U.S. Office of Scientific Research and Development during WWII. Best known in knowledge management contexts for his 1945 essay "As We May Think," which introduced the concept of the **Memex** — a hypothetical personal knowledge device that stored and allowed navigation of documents via associative trails.

Bush's vision was of a private, actively curated store of knowledge where the connections between documents were as valuable as the documents themselves. This anticipates the hypertext web, but also — more closely — the LLM Wiki pattern.

## Key Facts

- Proposed the Memex concept in "As We May Think," Atlantic Monthly, July 1945. [[wiki/sources/llm-wiki-idea]]
- The Memex was envisioned as a desk-sized device using microfilm, not digital storage.
- Bush identified that the problem with knowledge was not storage but *navigation* — following associative trails rather than artificial index hierarchies.
