# BetterUp

**Type:** org
**Also known as:** BetterUp, BetterUp Labs

## Overview

BetterUp is a coaching and workplace-performance company that appears in this wiki through research on AI's effects inside day-to-day desk work. In the current source set, its significance is not as a software product but as one of the organizations associated with the study that helped coin the term [[wiki/concepts/workslop]].

The article names Stanford researcher Jeff Hancock as both a co-author of the workslop study and a BetterUp scientific adviser. That makes BetterUp relevant here as part of the emerging research-and-advisory layer around AI's workplace effects, especially the gap between productivity claims from leadership and correction burdens experienced by workers.

## Key Facts

- A BetterUp-linked study cited in *The Guardian* helped coin the term "workslop" for polished-looking but flawed AI output [[wiki/sources/ai-productivity-workplace-errors]]
- Jeff Hancock is identified in the article as a Stanford researcher and BetterUp scientific adviser [[wiki/sources/ai-productivity-workplace-errors]]
- The cited study is not yet peer-reviewed, so BetterUp's appearance in the wiki should be treated as analytically useful but still provisional [[wiki/sources/ai-productivity-workplace-errors]]
