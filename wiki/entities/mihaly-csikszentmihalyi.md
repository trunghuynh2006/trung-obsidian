# Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi

**Type:** person
**Field:** Positive psychology
**Era:** 1934–2021

## Overview

Hungarian-American psychologist; professor at University of Chicago, later Claremont Graduate University. Best known for identifying and studying the concept of **flow** — the state of complete absorption in a challenging activity. His 1990 book *Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience* brought the concept to mainstream attention.

Csikszentmihalyi spent decades studying people across domains — rock climbers, surgeons, chess players, musicians, artists — to identify what conditions produce peak experience and intrinsic motivation. The nine characteristics of flow emerged from this cross-domain research.

## Key Contributions

- Coined and defined the concept of **flow state** [[wiki/concepts/flow-state]].
- Identified the nine characteristics of flow through empirical research.
- Established the challenge-skill balance as the central condition for flow entry.
- *Flow: The Psychology of Optimal Experience* (1990) — foundational text.
- Pronunciation: "cheek-sent-me-hi."
