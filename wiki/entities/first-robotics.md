# FIRST Robotics

**Type:** organization / competition
**Audience:** primarily high school students

## Overview

A competitive robotics program where student teams design, build, and program robots to compete in annual game challenges. Teams work under time pressure with real engineering constraints — mechanical, electrical, and software all at once.

Recommended as the best shortcut for high schoolers interested in robotics because it forces hands-on building, breaking, and iterating with real deadlines. The competitive environment and team structure mirror professional engineering more closely than any course or kit alone.

Related programs in the same ecosystem: VEX Robotics, RoboFest.

## Key Facts

- Forces cross-disciplinary work (mechanical, electrical, software) under deadline. [[wiki/sources/robotics-getting-started]]
- Teaches iteration: build → test → break → fix, with competition as the forcing function.
- Accessible to high schoolers without prior engineering background.
