# Cal Newport

**Type:** person
**Role:** Author, Associate Professor of Computer Science, Georgetown University

## Overview

Computer scientist and author known for applying research on focus, skill development, and deliberate practice to knowledge work and career strategy. Most influential for coining and popularizing **deep work** — distraction-free, cognitively demanding work that creates value and builds skill.

Newport's work bridges academic research (flow, attention, deliberate practice) and practical professional advice. He is also notable for his critique of social media and the attention economy as structural threats to knowledge worker productivity.

## Key Works

- *Deep Work: Rules for Focused Success in a Distracted World* (2016) — defines deep work vs. shallow work; argues focused concentration is the key skill of the modern economy.
- *So Good They Can't Ignore You* (2012) — career capital theory; skill-first over passion-first approach.
- *Digital Minimalism* (2019) — intentional use of technology; pushback against always-on connectivity.
- *A World Without Email* (2021) — critique of the hyperactive hive mind in workplace communication.
