# Adam Smith

**Type:** person

## Overview

Adam Smith (1723–1790) was a Scottish moral philosopher and the founder of classical economics. His *The Theory of Moral Sentiments* (1759) examined the friction between individual self-interest and societal norms; *The Wealth of Nations* (1776) laid the groundwork for modern market economics. Freakonomics invokes him as a precedent: Smith was first a philosopher who became an economist by asking how human behavior and incentives shape social outcomes — the same project Levitt and Dubner undertake with modern data.

## Key Facts

- Author of *The Theory of Moral Sentiments* (1759) and *The Wealth of Nations* (1776) [[wiki/sources/freakonomics-introduction]]
- Studied the friction between individual desire and societal norms — Freakonomics frames itself as a continuation of this project [[wiki/sources/freakonomics-introduction]]
- Believed the key to understanding moral behavior was an "impartial observer" perspective — Levitt uses this as a framing for Freakonomics' approach
