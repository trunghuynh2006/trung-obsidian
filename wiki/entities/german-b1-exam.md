# German B1 Exam

**Type:** certification (language)
**Also known as:** Goethe-Zertifikat B1, Telc Deutsch B1, ÖSD B1

## Overview

The German B1 exam certifies intermediate German proficiency under the Common European Framework of Reference (CEFR). At B1 level, a learner can handle familiar situations, describe experiences and events, give brief reasons for opinions, and communicate in straightforward language on topics relating to work, school, leisure, and daily life — without fluency, but with enough range to sustain a conversation.

The most widely recognized versions are:
- **Goethe-Zertifikat B1** — administered by the Goethe-Institut; internationally recognized; required for some German residency and citizenship pathways
- **Telc Deutsch B1** — recognized for German citizenship (Einbürgerungstest context), work permits
- **ÖSD Zertifikat B1** — Austrian provider; recognized across German-speaking countries

All three versions test the same CEFR competencies across the same four skill areas, with minor format differences.

## Exam Structure (Goethe-Zertifikat B1)

| Component | Skills | Duration | Weight |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lesen (Reading) | Comprehension across 3–4 text types | 65 min | 25% |
| Hören (Listening) | Comprehension across 3–4 audio types | 40 min | 25% |
| Schreiben (Writing) | Formal/semi-formal letter or message | 60 min | 25% |
| Sprechen (Speaking) | Discussion with a partner; presentation | 15 min | 25% |

Each component is graded independently. A minimum score is required per component — failing one component fails the exam even if overall average is sufficient.

## Key Language Competencies Tested

**Grammar:**
- Verb conjugation: present, simple past (Präteritum), present perfect (Perfekt), future
- Modal verbs (können, müssen, dürfen, sollen, wollen, mögen) in all tenses
- Subordinate clause word order (verb-final): *weil, dass, obwohl, wenn, als, ob*
- Relative clauses with correct case agreement
- Adjective endings (nominative/accusative/dative/genitive × 3 genders × definite/indefinite/no article)
- Separable and inseparable verbs
- Passive voice (Vorgangspassiv and Zustandspassiv)
- Konjunktiv II for politeness and hypotheticals (*würde*, *könnte*, *hätte*, *wäre*)
- Two-way prepositions with accusative vs. dative
- Genitive case in formal writing

**Vocabulary:**
- ~2,500–3,000 word families (B1 productive range)
- Topic domains: work, education, health, travel, environment, media, housing, personal relationships
- Connectives and discourse markers for written and spoken coherence

**Writing task:**
- Semi-formal letter or email (complaint, request, invitation, response)
- Clear structure: greeting, body paragraphs, closing
- Appropriate register (not overly formal, not casual)
- ~100–150 words

**Speaking tasks:**
- Presentation of a topic from a prompt card (~2 minutes)
- Discussion with a partner or examiner (~5 minutes)
- Expressing and justifying opinions, reacting to what the partner says
