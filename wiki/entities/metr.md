# METR

**Type:** organization
**Also known as:** Model Evaluation and Threat Research
**Founded:** 2023 (spun out of another AI safety nonprofit)
**Location:** Berkeley, CA (co-working space shared with other AI safety groups; AI Futures Project is one floor above)

## Overview

METR is a 30-person nonprofit dedicated to credible, third-party evaluation of frontier AI models. Its stated goal is to provide the public and policymakers with reliable measures of AI capability and risk before those capabilities mature into consequences. Funding comes primarily from private philanthropies (including the Audacious Project); the major AI labs provide free compute credits in exchange for pre-release model testing.

METR is best known for the **time-horizon chart** — a benchmark tracking the length of software engineering task (measured in human expert hours) that an AI agent can complete reliably. That trend line has become the most closely watched single data series in the AI industry, used by researchers, investors, and executives to gauge the pace of AI progress.

## Key Research Lines

- **Time-horizon evaluation** — measures task-completion length for AI agents on software engineering and AI research tasks; currently doubling every 3–4 months.
- **Covert capabilities research** — tests whether AI systems can misbehave surreptitiously while an AI monitor watches; an early form of containment research.
- **Productivity trials** — randomized controlled experiments measuring whether AI tools actually speed up developers (results have swung from −19% to +20% in successive studies).
