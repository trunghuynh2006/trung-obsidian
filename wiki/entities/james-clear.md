# James Clear

**Type:** person
**Role:** Author, habit researcher

## Overview

Author of *Atomic Habits* (2018), one of the best-selling books on habit formation and behavior change. Clear synthesizes behavioral science research into a practical framework for building good habits and breaking bad ones. Central to his work is the mathematics of marginal gains: small consistent improvements compound dramatically over time.

## Key Ideas

- **1% rule**: A 1% improvement per day compounds to a 37× improvement over one year. A 1% decline compounds to near zero. The direction of small daily habits matters enormously over long time horizons.
- **Habit stacking**: Link new habits to existing behaviors. "After I [current habit], I will [new habit]." The existing behavior becomes the trigger; the new behavior becomes the response.
- **Systems over goals**: Goals set direction; systems determine progress. You don't rise to the level of your goals — you fall to the level of your systems.
- **Identity-based habits**: The most effective behavior change starts with identity ("I am the kind of person who..."), not outcomes ("I want to...").
- **Four laws of behavior change**: Make it obvious, make it attractive, make it easy, make it satisfying.
